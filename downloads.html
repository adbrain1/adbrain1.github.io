For Android phones

Three short steps. About 2 minutes total.

01
Open

Open Chrome on your phone

Tap the Chrome app icon to open it. If you don't have Chrome, install it free from the Play Store first.

  1. Find the Chrome app on your phone (a coloured circle with a blue dot in the middle).
  2. Tap it to open.
  3. In the address bar at the top, type or paste:
    www.rafaytravelsworldwide.com/chat-app.html
  4. Tap Go or Enter.
02
Install

Add the app to your home screen

Now Chrome will help you turn this page into a real app icon on your home screen.

  1. At the top right of Chrome, tap the menu button ( ⋮ three dots).
  2. Tap Install app (or Add to Home Screen — it may say either).
  3. A small box appears. Tap Install or Add.
  4. Wait a moment — you'll see a new R icon on your home screen.
  5. Tap the new R icon to open the app.
Don't see "Install app"? Try tapping ⋮ → Add to Home Screen. Some Chrome versions phrase it differently — both work the same.
03
Enter

Sign in with your name

  1. The app asks for your name. Type your name (e.g. "Naghmana") and tap Join Chat.
  2. A pop-up appears asking "Allow notifications?" — tap Allow. This is what lets your phone buzz when there's a new message.
  3. You'll only be asked once. The next time you open the app, you go straight in.

Make sure notifications always work

Android phones save battery by silencing apps in the background. Do these one-time steps so you never miss a message.

04
Important

Turn off battery savings for Chrome

This step is the single biggest reason messages get missed. It's a one-time setting.

  1. Open your phone's Settings app (the gear icon).
  2. Tap Apps (or Apps & notifications).
  3. Scroll down and tap Chrome.
  4. Tap Battery.
  5. Choose Unrestricted (or "No restrictions" / "Don't optimise" — the wording varies by phone).
Samsung phones — Settings → Apps → Chrome → Battery → set to Unrestricted. Then also: Settings → Battery and device care → Battery → Background usage limits → make sure Chrome is not in the "Sleeping apps" list.
Xiaomi / Redmi / POCO phones — Settings → Apps → Manage apps → Chrome → Battery saver → No restrictions. Then: Settings → Battery → App battery saver → Chrome → No restrictions.
Huawei / Honor — Settings → Battery → App launch → Chrome → switch off "Manage automatically", then turn on all three options shown (Auto-launch, Secondary launch, Run in background).
05
Allow

Make sure notifications are switched on

  1. Open Settings → Apps → Chrome.
  2. Tap Notifications.
  3. Make sure the main switch at the top is ON.
  4. Scroll down — make sure Sites is turned on. If you see rafaytravelsworldwide.com listed, make sure that's ON too.
  5. If your phone offers a "Priority" or "Important" notification category, set Rafay Chat to Priority / High.
06
Optional

Don't sleep, Chrome

Some phones aggressively "put apps to sleep" if you haven't opened them in a while. Make sure Chrome isn't sleeping.

  1. Settings → Battery (or "Battery & device care").
  2. Look for Sleeping apps, App standby, or Deep sleep apps.
  3. If you see Chrome in any of these lists, tap it and remove it.
Good to know: After these settings, your phone will buzz reliably even if the app isn't open. Notifications use the same system as WhatsApp.

For iPhone & iPad

Three short steps. About 2 minutes total. You'll need iOS 16.4 or newer for notifications to work.

01
Open

Open Safari (not Chrome)

This is important: on iPhone, the install only works in Apple's Safari browser. Chrome or other browsers won't work for this.

  1. Find the Safari app on your phone (a blue compass icon).
  2. Tap to open it.
  3. In the address bar, type or paste:
    www.rafaytravelsworldwide.com/chat-app.html
  4. Tap Go.
02
Install

Add to Home Screen

  1. At the bottom of Safari (or top, on iPad), tap the Share button — a square with an arrow pointing up: Share
  2. Scroll down the menu that appears and tap Add to Home Screen.
  3. Tap Add in the top-right corner.
  4. You'll see a new R icon on your home screen.
  5. Tap the new R icon to open the app.
Can't find "Add to Home Screen"? Make sure you tapped the proper Share button (square with up-arrow), not something else. Scroll down in that menu — it's not always at the top.
03
Enter

Sign in & allow notifications

  1. The app asks for your name. Type your name (e.g. "Sarah") and tap Join Chat.
  2. A pop-up will say "Rafay Chat would like to send you notifications" — tap Allow.
  3. You'll only be asked once. Next time you open it, you go straight in.

Make sure notifications always work

iPhone is much more reliable than Android by default, but these small checks make sure you don't miss anything.

04
Important

Check iPhone notifications are switched on

  1. Open the Settings app.
  2. Scroll down and tap Notifications.
  3. Scroll through the apps list and find Rafay Chat.
  4. Tap it. Make sure Allow Notifications is ON.
  5. Underneath, check that Sounds and Badges are on.
  6. For "Show in Notification Centre" and "Lock Screen", make sure both are ticked.
05
Optional

Turn off "Focus" mode for Rafay Chat

If you use Focus modes (e.g. Sleep, Work, Do Not Disturb), notifications can get hidden. Allow Rafay Chat through.

  1. Settings → Focus.
  2. Tap each Focus mode you use (e.g. "Do Not Disturb", "Sleep").
  3. Under Allowed Apps, add Rafay Chat.
Update needed? Notifications require iOS 16.4 or later. Open Settings → General → Software Update to check. Most iPhones from 2017 and newer support this update.
